aetas

The aetas project is a compact device designed to fit within a physical planner, equipped with a sensitive touchscreen sensor that detects handwritten entries in real time. the device captures this written information, converts it into digital text, and automatically syncs it with an online calendar, such as Google Calendar.


The goal of this project is to provide a streamlined and user-friendly solution for the individuals who rely on both physical and digital planning systems. by merging the traditional with the modern, this device offers a unique way to simplify scheduling, improve accuracy, and enhance productivity. Whether for busy professionals, students, or anyone juggling multiple commitmemts, this device has the potential to transform how people organize their lives.




highlights:





- a precise sensor that will detect writing.
- two dials indicating the day and month for the user to determine when their event will be held.
- the mobile app will allow users to link their gmail to add their entries.
- the app will communicate with the device via bluetooth and will process the data received. this processing will result in the user's handwriting being turned to text and added to their google calendar.
